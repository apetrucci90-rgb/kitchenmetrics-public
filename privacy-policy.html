Privacy Policy — KitchenMetrics

App: KitchenMetrics — Professional Cooking Suite (com.kitchenmetrics.kitchentools)
Effective: 12 July 2026 · Last updated: 12 July 2026

Summary: KitchenMetrics does not collect, transmit or share any personal data. Everything you enter stays on your device. No accounts, no analytics, no advertising, no tracking.

1. Data we collect

None. KitchenMetrics does not collect personal or sensitive user data. It does not ask for your name, email, phone number, location, contacts, photos, or any device identifier. There is no sign-in.

2. Data stored on your device

So you can resume work after closing the app, KitchenMetrics saves the values you type into its calculators — ingredient prices, yields, portion weights, equipment settings, cost parameters — locally, in Android's private app storage (SharedPreferences).

  • This data never leaves your device. It is not uploaded and not shared.
  • It is private to the app and unreadable by other apps.
  • Uninstalling KitchenMetrics deletes it. You may also clear it via Android Settings → Apps → KitchenMetrics → Storage → Clear data.

3. Network use

KitchenMetrics contacts exactly one external service:

  • USDA FoodData Central (api.nal.usda.gov), run by the United States Department of Agriculture. When you use the Thermal Cooking Calculator, the app may request the public nutritional composition of the food profile you selected.

These requests contain only a public food identifier (for example, the catalogue number for "chicken breast"). They contain no personal data, no device identifiers, and nothing you typed into the app. All requests use HTTPS. If the request fails or you are offline, the app falls back to its built-in database and keeps working.

USDA FoodData Central is a third party with its own privacy practices, which we do not control.

4. Data we share

None. We do not sell, rent or share data with anyone — we do not collect any.

5. Analytics, advertising and tracking

KitchenMetrics contains no analytics SDK, no advertising SDK and no crash-reporting SDK. It does not track you across apps or websites, and builds no profile of you.

6. Permissions

KitchenMetrics requests a single Android permission:

  • INTERNET — used only for the optional USDA lookup described in section 3.

It requests no access to location, camera, microphone, contacts, files or storage.

7. Children

KitchenMetrics is a professional tool for kitchen and hospitality work. It is not directed at children and does not knowingly collect data from anyone, including children.

8. Your rights

Because we hold no personal data about you on any server, there is nothing for us to export, correct or delete. The data on your device is entirely under your control.

9. Changes

If this policy changes, the updated version will be published at this address and the "Last updated" date revised.
